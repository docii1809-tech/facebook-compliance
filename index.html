Common Facebook Community Standards Violations

Understanding these categories helps you navigate Facebook’s automated moderation filters:

  • Hate Speech: Attacking people based on protected characteristics (race, religion, etc.).
  • Harassment & Bullying: Content that targets individuals to degrade or shame them.
  • Violence & Threats: Language that incites violence or promotes illegal acts.
  • Adult Content: Excessive sexual language or non-consensual imagery.
  • Spam & Engagement Bait: Explicitly asking for likes, shares, or comments to "game" the algorithm.
  • Misinformation: False claims, especially regarding health (COVID-19, vaccines) or elections.

Why Facebook Removes Posts

  • Automated Moderation: AI bots scan text for "trigger words" instantly.
  • User Reports: If users flag your content, a human moderator may review it.
  • Ad Policy Overlap: Organic posts that look like "unapproved ads" (e.g., weight loss claims) are often suppressed.
  • Repeat Violations: Multiple strikes can lead to "Shadowbanning" or page deletion.

How to Avoid Facebook Post Removals

  • Avoid absolute claims: Instead of "This WILL make you rich," use "How I improved my finances."
  • Don’t target protected classes: Use inclusive, neutral language.
  • Use neutral language: Avoid overly aggressive or sensationalist headlines.
  • Add disclosures: If you are being paid to promote a product, always use the Paid Partnership tag.
